Here is a look at what's on the menu for our first newsletter of 2026: the training sessions and projects built around eCorpus that have kept us busy since the last issue, customizable lighting in Voyager scenes, updated documentation on user permissions, and a behind-the-scenes look at the development of new features!

<h3>eCorpus events and projects</h3>

<p>
eCorpus development and promotion is gradually becoming part of the mission of a <b>3D Competence Center</b> founded by the University of Lille and Holusion. The official launch is planned for the first quarter of 2026, with the goal of supporting eCorpus development, providing training in photogrammetry, and helping institutions digitize cultural and scientific heritage.
</p>

<p>Here's a closer look at four projects and events we took part in at the end of 2025.</p>

<h4>Digital Heritage Forum, Abu Dhabi</h4>

<p>
We were invited to the very first <b>Digital Heritage Forum</b> in the United Arab Emirates, held November 3–5, 2025, to host a roundtable on collaborative digital spaces. With six speakers from Europe, Africa, and Asia, our goal was to highlight the key challenges around 3D data for heritage—especially in an international context where researchers, governments, institutions, local communities, and NGOs often come together with sometimes diverging goals.
</p>

<img src="/assets/img/post/Newsletter_05_abuDhabi.jpg" alt="Digital Heritage Forum" />

<h4>Photogrammetry training at Le Fresnoy</h4>

<p>
In collaboration with CNRS and the Science and Visual Culture Research Federation, we led our first <a href="https://pepr-iccare.fr/events/formation-initiation-a-la-photogrammetrie/">training session on photogrammetry and using eCorpus</a>. The workshop took place as part of <a href="https://pepr-iccare.fr/">PEPR ICCARE</a>, an ambitious research program bringing together academic labs and the Cultural and Creative Industries. Designed for researchers and collection specialists, the training was held under real-world conditions thanks to the participation of the museum La Piscine in Roubaix.
</p>

<p>
The program covered photogrammetry theory, free and open-source software for 3D reconstruction, studio photography, and on-site capture in exhibition galleries. All participants were then able to upload their work to an eCorpus database to enrich and share it.
</p>

<img src="/assets/img/post/Newsletter_05_faucheur.jpg" alt="eCorpus scene of The Old Worker" />

<p>A second training session will take place in 2026.</p>

<h4>Secondary education and fine crafts</h4>

<p>
In the Hauts-de-France region, the <a href="https://www.culture.gouv.fr/regions/drac-hauts-de-france/contacter-la-drac-hauts-de-france/les-lyceens-et-apprentis-des-hauts-de-france-en-route-vers-la-villa-medicis">Villa Médicis project</a> was launched on November 13 at the Louvre-Lens museum, in the presence of Xavier Bertrand, the President of the region. Ten schools offering vocational training in fine crafts will create masterworks to be showcased during an artistic residency at the iconic Villa Médicis in Rome in spring 2026.
</p>

<p>
To preserve a digital record of these works, the region will use eCorpus to digitize, enrich, and share 3D models of woodworking, stone carving, ornamental ironwork, and other creations by these young talents.
</p>

<h4>PEPR ICCARE Day</h4>

<p>
On December 16, the Musée de l'Hospice Comtesse in Lille hosted an <a href="https://pepr-iccare.fr/events/revoir-et-revivre-pour-transmettre-les-technologies-immersives-au-service-du-patrimoine-et-des-musees/">acceleration day focused on immersive technologies</a> for heritage and museums. Automated 3D scanning studios, 3D reproduction, holograms, mediation tools—our aim was to showcase the many facets of 3D digitization and highlight the importance of proper documentation and long-term preservation.
</p>

<p>
A mediation application for the <b>Coronelli globes</b>, developed using eCorpus, was unveiled at the event. Its installation in the museum galleries is planned for later this year, allowing visitors to explore both the celestial and the terrestrial globe in detail.
</p>

<img src="/assets/img/post/Newsletter_05_pepr.jpg" alt="eCorpus application for the Coronelli Globes" />
